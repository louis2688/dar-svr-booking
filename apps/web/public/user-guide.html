What this system is for

The Service Vehicle Request (SVR) system is how staff of the DAR Provincial Office in Marinduque ask to use an office vehicle for official travel.

Instead of paper slips, you fill out a short online form. An administrator reviews it, assigns a vehicle, and approves it. You can then print the official request form, and everyone can see which vehicles are already booked on a given day.

  • Regular users can create requests, follow their status, and view the shared calendar.
  • Administrators can do all of that plus assign vehicles, approve or reject requests, and manage the office records.
2

Signing in & your account

Open svr-booking-dar.vercel.app in any web browser, or use the mobile app (see section 9).

If you already have an account

  1. Tap Sign in.
  2. Enter your email and password.
  3. Tap Sign in again — you'll land on your dashboard.

If you're new

  1. Tap Sign up on the welcome screen.
  2. Enter your name, work email, and a password you'll remember.
  3. Check your email for a verification link and open it to activate your account.
Forgot your password? On the sign-in screen choose Forgot password, enter your email, and follow the reset link we send you. Your old password stops working once you set a new one.
3

Reading the dashboard

The dashboard is your home screen. From the menu on the left you can reach Dashboard, Bookings, Calendar, and New Request.

At the top are four count cards — Pending, Approved, Rejected, and Cancelled — followed by a chart of the year and a table of recent bookings.

Good to know The dashboard shows all bookings in the office, not only your own, so you can see the whole picture. Only administrators see the Edit, Cancel, and Delete buttons — for everyone else it's view-only.
4

Requesting a vehicle

Choose New Request from the menu, then work down the form.

  1. Pick the trip date on the calendar. Grey past dates can't be chosen.
  2. Choose a start time. Times already taken show (booked) and can't be picked — choose a free one.
  3. Enter the requestor's name — the person the vehicle is for.
  4. Type the destination and the purpose of travel.
  5. Add passengers, one name per line (optional).
  6. Tap Submit request. You'll get a control number — write it down or take a screenshot.
What happens next Your request starts as Pending. An administrator assigns a vehicle and either approves or rejects it. You don't pick the vehicle yourself — that's done during approval.
5

What the statuses mean

Every request carries one status, shown as a coloured label:

Pending

Submitted and waiting for an administrator to review and assign a vehicle.

Approved

A vehicle is assigned and the trip is confirmed. You can print the form.

Rejected

The request was declined. Submit a new one if plans change.

Cancelled

The booking was called off by an administrator.

You can filter the Bookings list by status, search by control number or destination, sort the list, and choose how many rows to show per page.

6

The booking calendar

Choose Calendar to see, at a glance, which vehicles are busy. Pick a vehicle (or leave it on All vehicles) and a month.

Reading a day

  • All vehicles: a day shows how many bookings it has, e.g. 3 booked.
  • One vehicle: a day shows a number like 1/48. The day is split into 48 half-hour start times; 1/48 means one of them is taken.
  • Colours: green means open, amber means partly booked, red means fully booked.

Tap any day to see the exact times booked, along with the vehicle and control number for each.

7

Viewing & printing a form

From the dashboard or Bookings list, tap any row to open its official Request of Vehicle form. Then tap Print / Save as PDF.

The sheet is sized for long bond (Folio, 8.5×13 in) and prints two copies per page with a cut line between them.

Before you print In the print window, turn off "Headers and footers" so the date and web address don't appear on the paper.

10

For administrators

Administrators see extra menu items: Approvals, Vehicles, Signatories, and Activity History.

Approving a request

  1. Open Approvals to see all pending requests.
  2. Assign a vehicle to the request.
  3. Choose Approve or Reject.

Editing, cancelling, deleting

On any booking row, administrators can Edit details, Cancel it, or Delete it. These actions are admin-only.

Control numbers

Each booking gets a control number automatically. It looks like this:

2026Year – 07Month – 0229Number ALetter

The letter cycles A, B, C … Z and back to A with each new booking. When entering an old paper record, an administrator can type the control number by hand instead — including on the printed form itself.

Past bookings

Administrators may pick past dates to record trips that already happened. Regular users can only book today or later.

Managing the office records

  • Vehicles — add, rename, or retire vehicles.
  • Signatories — set the names, positions, and signature images printed on the form.
  • Activity History — a running log of who created, approved, rejected, or cancelled each booking, and when.
11

Common questions

Can two trips share the same vehicle and time?

The form won't let you pick a time that's already booked, to avoid mistakes. If a genuine shared trip is needed, an administrator can arrange it.

Why can't I choose a vehicle when requesting?

By design — the administrator assigns the vehicle when approving, based on what's available.

I can see other people's bookings. Is that right?

Yes. Everyone can view all bookings so the office schedule is transparent. Only administrators can change them.

What time zone are the dates and times?

Everything is shown in Manila time (Asia/Manila).
